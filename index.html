2. Core Proof: Complex-Conjugate Optimality & Sim-Verified Data

The NARK design is based on the foundational hydrodynamic equation, where maximum efficiency is achieved by tuning the device's internal mechanical impedance ($\boldsymbol{Z_{mech}}$) to perfectly cancel the external radiation reactance ($\boldsymbol{Z_{rad}}$).

2.1. The Control Law: Impedance Matching

The core principle of **Complex-Conjugate Control** requires the total system impedance to match the conjugate of the hydrodynamic impedance. This results in the following component-wise demands for the controller:

Principle of Complex-Conjugate Optimality

The control system must dynamically apply stiffness and damping to make the mechanical impedance ($Z_{mech}$) the complex conjugate of the hydrodynamic impedance ($Z_{hydro}$).

$$ Z_{control}(\omega) = Z^{*}_{radiation}(\omega) \implies \begin{cases} K_{ctrl}(\omega) = m_A(\omega) \omega^2 - K_{hydro} \\ B_{ctrl}(\omega) = B_{rad}(\omega) \end{cases} $$ The NARK platform's control unit applies $\boldsymbol{K_{ctrl}}$ (stiffness) and $\boldsymbol{B_{ctrl}}$ (damping) via the alternator's electrical field, dynamically satisfying this equality to maintain resonance.
